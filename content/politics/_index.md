## The State Is the Biggest Industry

**Every policy debate eventually arrives at the same question: should the government do this? Max Weber asks a different one: who told you the government could do it, and what do they get out of the answer?**

This section looks at power - who has it, who wants it, who profits from expanding it, and who pays when it fails. No conspiracy theories. Just incentive structures.

---

- **[The Sock Puppet Industry →](/politics/sock-puppet-industry/)** — Your taxes flow through a pipeline of quangos and committees to pay charities that lobby for more government. Most of the money never reaches the cause. That is not a bug. It is the system.

- **[The Seven Questions →](/politics/seven-questions/)** — Seven questions every law should answer before it passes. Almost none would survive. That is the point.

- **[The Battle of Ideas →](/politics/battle-of-ideas/)** — The real fight is not left vs right. It is between those who believe power should be questioned and those who believe it should be trusted. Most people are fighting on the wrong battlefield.

- **[Democracy's Impossible Voter →](/politics/democracys-impossible-voter/)** — Democracy assumes voters are informed, rational, and engaged. The evidence says they are none of these things. The gap between the assumption and the reality is where the power lives.

- **[Free Speech in Britain →](/politics/free-speech-in-britain/)** — Britain is losing its commitment to free speech through death by a thousand regulations, not a single censorship law. The Online Safety Act, the counter-extremism duty, and police guidance on non-crime hate incidents have created a permission structure for silencing speech without ever admitting that is what is happening.

- **[Locke's Ghost →](/politics/lockes-ghost/)** — What does it mean to have a right if the government can override it whenever it decides the circumstances are exceptional? The emergency exception has become the rule.

- **[The Grievance Machine, Part II: Who Profits from Panic →](/politics/the-grievance-machine-part-ii/)** — The grievance machine does not care about the problem. It cares about the panic. Panic is profitable. The hyenas do not want the crisis solved — they want it sustained.

- **[The Labour PM Who Cut Your Climate Tax →](/politics/burnham-energy-tax-cut/)** — Labour's energy policy in 2026 is a paradox: cut the climate levy while pushing harder into net zero. The reconciliation comes from the politics, not the economics.

- **[The Seven Questions Applied: Net Zero →](/politics/seven-questions-net-zero/)** — Applying the Seven Questions framework to net zero policy. What is the goal? Who wins? Who loses? How will we know if it is working? The answers reveal more than the advocates want you to see.
